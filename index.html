About

Built on
Engineering
Fundamentals.

Riley Dierker founded Dierker Precision Machining after completing a degree in Manufacturing Engineering at Western Washington University — one of the country's premier engineering programs.

With a deep understanding of material properties, machining theory, and quality control, Riley brings an engineer's rigor to every job. This isn't just cutting metal — it's applying science to produce components that perform exactly as designed.

Whether you need a single prototype or a small production run, every part is held to the same uncompromising standard: if the print says ±0.001", it will hit ±0.001".

Materials
